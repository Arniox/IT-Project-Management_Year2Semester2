Team Gantt
Pros:
Cons:
  • A Gantt chart organizes activities. It lists tasks and subtasks in logical groupings. It also shows who is responsible for each, and dates for starting and completing each task.
  • The visual format is easy to read and understand. Gantt charts give managers and team members a visual overview of when activities will occur, and whether they are dependent upon completion of other tasks.
  • It helps to set reasonable time frames. Breaking down activities into detail and assigning a due date for each creates a better, more accurate forecast of when the entire project will be complete.
  • It can become too complex for larger projects. A good project management software program, such as SmartDraw, won't have trouble handling a large project. However, if you find that your project is too large for your Gantt chart to function well, consider breaking it into multiple Gantt charts.
  • It can be somewhat inflexible. This is a complaint commonly heard from managers who use inflexible project management programs, or try to create their own Gantt charts by hand or using a spreadsheet. SmartDraw lets you view and edit your Gantt chart in a mind map view. Any changes you make in one view will be also update the other view, automatically, thus alleviating the inflexibility issue.
  • It must be constantly updated. Projects involve surprises and changes must be made. SmartDraw is designed to make these changes as quick and effortless as possible.

Key Feature:
Initiation:
Trello's initiation isn't very fleshed out. There's no specific team member hierarchy, no charting, calander or automatic documentation. Trello is more like a virtual pin board where weekly tasks can be created and pinned to the lists.
To initiate a project, first a board needs to be created. Afterwards, members are invited and the project can begin

Initiation:
Teamgantt's initiation is built around the gantt chart and so the initiation phase is quite fleshed out. Teamgantt's member system is also quite fleshed out.
You can create a user (defined by: "any individual invited to the teamgantt account by email address regardless of permission"), a guest (a view only member with basic user account level permissions) and a resource (a group or non-user to teamgantt for scheduling purposes only.)

Planning:
For planning, trello uses a hierarchy system. You first create a board which represents your entire project. Inside that, you create lists. These represent sections/sprints/groups or phases of a project. One list might be for week 1 development, and another for week 2 development. Inside the lists, you create cards. Each individual card represents either a data or informative piece of info, or a task that someone must complete. Trello does not contain any network diagram system or gantt chart integration.

Planning:
Planning with Teamgantt is very different to trello and starts by creating a chart. The empty chart is then populated by phases. Each phase is a section of your project. The child tasks of the phases are individual tasks, or grouped tasts. Grouped tasks contain many sub tasks that all come under a short time period.
Teamgantt is built around the concept of the gantt chart and thus all tasks, diagrams, links and team member monitoring are within the chart

Execution:
Trello does not have native support for normal project management systems. It does not have kick off meeting systems, miletones and so on. Trello is more built for the casual project management system and not a business. It's very manual intensive and to create documents, link things and to show progression and execution, you need to manually update and create everything.
Trello also doesn't have any timeline charting or networking implementation.

Execution:
With teamgantt, there is heaps of built in support for kick-off meetings, collaborating, team meeting records, milestones and so on. Executing is just a mater of starting the project and letting teamgantt mostly plan everything out. Once you create tasks and milestones, meetings and such are auto implemented into your project

Monitoring/Control:
Trello's list/card system for monitoring is extremely well layed out. You can create labels which can be stacked infinitely on a card and gives a task a lot of definition in a very clear and concise way. Each card can be opened and inside, the team can insert information, links, lists, descriptions and even sub tasks.
For a casual project management system at a very small price, Trello perfectly fits the bill

Monitoring/Control:
Teamgantt's monitoring and control is much more suited to a big business. Disregarding the actual timeline and networking diagrams themselves, Teamgantt also has automatic email updates, markdown formatting, embeded projects, progress updates, dependencies, subgroups, templates, workloads, % of completion, checklists, reporting and so on.
For monitoring and control, teamgantt has an abundance of resources to use to help control your project as a big business.

Closing:
Trello doesn't have any specific way to close or finish a project. The tasts can be moved around through different lists, and so a finished list can be created at the start of the project and as it progresses, cards are dropped in. But besides manually creating an artificial closing system, trello boards stay online permanently until they are deleted by the board creator.

Closing:
Teamgantt has built in auto documentation when the project is complete and has timeline tracking the whole way so you can see how fast the project was being completed during certain stages. The milestones act as a closing point since on reaching the milestone, reports can be requested and generated. If no other tasks exist after the final milestone, the project completes and final documentation is generated.

Based on all the pros and cons, as well as the compared key features shown above; our team gives a final review that the better project management system to use would be Trello as a student.
  • As a student, you don't need much detail like with Teamgantt and with R&D projects, as long as you are well planned, a gantt chart is not crucial and nore is a network diagram or many of the other things that come with Teamgannt
  • The university could much better fund the students if the project management system being used was Trello. Teamgantt's cost makes it an unwise choice to use especially since students do not need or require all of the indepth features of Teamgantt
